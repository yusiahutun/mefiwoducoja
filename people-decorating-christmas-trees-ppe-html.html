    People are decorating their Christmas trees with PPE

    While some will view Christmas as a time to escape from the horrors of 2020 and cuddle up with cup of hot chocolate and cheesy movie in front of bauble-laden tree. But others, perhaps those with a darker sense of humour, are not hiding from 2020 but instead decorating their trees with Covid-themed ornaments.

    22 Jun 2024, 00:00

    While some will view Christmas as a time to escape from the horrors of 2020 and cuddle up with cup of hot chocolate and cheesy movie in front of bauble-laden tree.

    But others, perhaps those with a darker sense of humour, are not hiding from 2020  but instead decorating their trees with Covid-themed ornaments.

    Taking to social media schools, dentist offices, shops and even family homes have shown off their Covid-themed trees which have been decorated with face masks, toilet roll, hand sanitiser and surgical gloves.

    One school in Scotland covered their festive fair with paper towels and toilet roll, while a radio station in Lancashire share a pictured of a tree adorned with masks, anti-bacterial wipes, pasta and tinned goods.

    Another GP surgery added blown up rubber gloves to their decorations.

    Meanwhile sellers of Etsy have put 'Covid vaccine' baubles up for sale.

    Here, FEMAIL reveals the best trees from the UK and the rest of the world...

    PP-Tree! One radio station in Lancashire shared a picture of a very festive tree - complete with washing up liquid, dried good and facemaskes

    A new take on tinsel! This school in Scotland adorned their tree with blue paper towels and toilet paper

    Dettol the halls! A GP surgery in Shettleston, Glasgow showed off their Zoflora themed clean tree

    Roll on Christmas! The trend appears to have crossed the pond with one user in  New York showing off their tissue tree

    Sanitiser season! One Instagram users in Canada put sanitiser, masks, and tissues on their festive offering 

    All wrapped up! Another user in Ashford Kent wrapped up their presents under their face-mask tree

    Loo woo! One Facebook user from the UK forwent the tree all together and decorated a mound of toilet roll

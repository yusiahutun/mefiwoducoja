Seattle's Ballard Firehouse Shutters

15 Jun 2024, 00:00

(CelebrityAccess News Service) — After 18 years of music, Ballard Firehouse, one of Seattle's most enduring and venerable nightclubs, shuttered its doors on December 28, 2003, after losing its lease. The venue, located in a historical firehouse, was a mainstay for local and nationally touring talent including music from the classic rock, folk, country and reggae worlds. Among those artists who have performed there, include Bad Company, Dokken, Kansas, John Entwistle, the Kinks' Dave Davies, UFO, Blue Oyster Cult, Jimmy Cliff, Kansas, the Fixx, the Romantics and Quiet Riot.

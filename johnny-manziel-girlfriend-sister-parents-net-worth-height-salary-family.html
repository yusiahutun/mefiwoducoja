    Johnny Manziel is a Lebanese-Italian-born quarterback of American football. Born and raised amid wealth, Johnny Manziel never lacked anything as a child. He had privileges that millions of children his age would never have in a lifetime. He has won many awards for his outstanding achievements in football and has broken many records that set the pace in American football. However, Johnny has failed to control his cocaine addiction, which has slowed his career somewhat and also led to him being suspended several times, ruining his chances of ever-blossoming in the game. He is currently a free agent.

    Johnny Manziel Bio

    Johnny Manziel was born on December 6, 1992, as Jonathan Paul Manziel in Tyler, Texas, the USA, the son of Paul Manziel and Michelle Liberato. His father made his fortune with the Texas oil refinery. Johnny Manziel is of Syrian descent and has American citizenship. He attended Tivy High School in Kerrville, Texas, and even as a young boy in high school he was enthusiastic about sports such as baseball, football, and golf. However, he was advised to stay with football because he had superhero abilities in the sport. Johnny played football for four years in high school. In his junior year, he started playing as a quarterback and finished the season with 2,903 passing yards, 1,544 rushing yards, 152 receiving yards, and 55 touchdowns. This achievement earned him the All-San Antonio Area Offensive Player of the Year award.

    In the senior national team, he far exceeded his previous achievements, and further awards and prizes followed. After high school, he received an athletic scholarship to A&M University, Texas, where he played quarterback for A&M Aggies. He broke records and gained popularity by becoming the first rookie to win the Heisman Trophy. But Johnny couldn’t control his fame as he was constantly getting into trouble and was briefly accused of signing autographs for money in 2013, which led to his suspension.

    Drug Addiction

    Johnny’s career went down the drain as fast as it started. He was only able to play two seasons in the National Football League (NFL) with the Cleveland Browns, who voted him into the 22nd overall NFL Draft 2014. Even though he had everything he could ever wish for, as his parents did everything they could to give him the best life possible, Johnny resorted to hard drugs and became an addict, which broke his parents’ hearts. He was sent to a rehab clinic to address the drug problem and spent 90 days there, but only a few weeks after his return he immediately returned to drugs and parties.

    Family, Parents, Sister

    johnny Manziel’s ancestors were immigrants to the United States of America from Lebanon Mountain. Joseph Manziel, the great-great-grandfather of Johnny Manziel, came to the USA in 1883 to survive. His son, Bobby Joe Manziel, who was a bantamweight boxer at the time, retired from boxing and decided to move to Texas to start a new life. He started drilling for oil and became a great success. Today his children and grandchildren still live on what he left them and continue to be in the oil business. Johnny’s grandfather and other relatives have long been charged with drug offenses, robbery, and alcohol consumption.

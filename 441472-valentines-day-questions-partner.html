    Valentine’s Day is that day of the year when love is felt and expressed at a higher level on a global scale. As much as it’s a day to celebrate love, it is also a day to reevaluate your relationship, and appreciate the milestones you have accomplished. Well-thought-out Valentine's Day questions will give you the chance to discover and learn more about your partner.

    People often get carried away with Valentine's Day preparations, be it booking a table for dinner, buying your partner the perfect gift or preparing the best date for this day. However, it is also essential to look at what makes the relationship healthy. Below is a list of Valentine’s Day questions that will provide a platform for a meaningful conversation on this special day.

    Valentine’s Day questions for couples

    Life can get busy at times, and it is significantly more challenging when you are in a relationship. Valentine’s Day is a day to focus on your partner and get to catch up and know each other more. For couples, it is vital to know each other at a deeper level to strengthen the relationship. Here is a list of Valentine’s Day questions for couples.

    Valentine’s day questions for your girlfriend

    Interestingly, the magnitude of anticipation for this day is stronger among ladies than it is among gents. It is the day their boyfriends project genuine love by sending flowers or other forms of gifts. Some are even taken out on dates. Below are questions for your girlfriend during this eventful day.

    • If you could change anything about Valentine’s day, what would you change?
    • Can I share with you the reasons why I love you so much?
    • What are three qualities about me that you were first attracted to?
    • What book has had the most significant impact on your life?
    • Would you rather have extraordinary love that doesn’t last forever or ordinary love that does?
    • Do you believe vulnerability is a good thing when it comes to love?
    • What’s one thing you would like to do less of and why? How can you make that happen? Is there anything I can do to support you?

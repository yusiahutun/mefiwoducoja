    • Ghetto Radio cancelled DJ Fatxo's interview that was scheduled on Friday, August 18, and the station explained it was due to fans' disapproval
    • The Mugithi singer was shocked by the statement and he argued that the interview was cancelled due to traffic delays
    • Fatxo claimed that the feedback from Ghetto Radio fans was positive; adding that there was no online uproar

    Mugithi singer Lawrence Njuguna Wagura, popularly known as DJ Fatxo, has clarified why his interview at Ghetto Radio was cancelled.

    In a statement which he shared on Facebook, DJ Fatxo refuted the claims that the station cancelled the show due to fans' uproar.

    Did fans disapprove Fatxo's interview?

    Ghetto Radio had clarified that they cancelled the interview because its fans found it in bad taste.

    They offered an apology to fans and explained that listeners come first.

    "Ghetto Radio cancelled an interview with Lawrence Wagura Njuguna, alias DJ Fatxo. That was scheduled on Friday, August 18, at 7am and 8am. This is due to an online uproar from our fans, who felt it was in bad taste. As an organisation, we value our customers and our listeners, you come fast," the organisation wrote in an official statement shared on Facebook.

    DJ Fatxo denies fan disapproval led to cancellation

    Responding to Ghetto Radio's statement, DJ Fatxo explained that the cancellation was due to traffic delays.

    He said there was no online uproar whatsoever from Ghetto Radio fans.

    In his words:

    "It has come to our attention that there is a letter doing rounds online that was ostensibly written by Ghetto Radio regarding the cancellation of DJ Fatxo's interview... Contrary to the assertion therein, the interview was not cancelled due to any online uproar from fans...

    The reality is that the cancellation was a result of unavoidable traffic delays. That caused DJ Fatxo to be late for the schedule interview. We therefore find the insinuation of that letter unwarranted and ill thought. It is distasteful misleading, and plainly just unfortunate."

    DJ Fatxo unmoved by claims

    He added that he has had several interviews with other local stations and the feedback was great.

    "DJ Fatxo has otherwise had successful interviews with Inooro and Citizen and performed in Gilgil, Naivasha Karatina and Kutus in just two weeks since coming back from a sabbatical..."

    DJ Fatxo proves he's innocent

    DJ Fatxo was linked to the death of Jeff Mwathi, who died in his house on February 22, 2023, under unclear circumstance.

    However, on Wednesday, May 17, DJ Fatxo was cleared because the Director of Public Prosecution (DPP) had failed to gather evidence to press murder charges against him.

    His lawyer, Okatch, said:

    "It is clear that there is no single shred or iota of evidence to suggest any remote criminal culpability by our client or suspicion thereof."

    He explained that Fatxo was not in the house when Jeff was reported to have fallen to his death in the said apartment.

    "In fact, it is very clear that our client was not even at his house at the material time that his friend, Jeff Mwathi met his death," he added.

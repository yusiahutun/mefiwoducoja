    It seems Julianne Hough was determined not to be outshone at her latest movie premiere.

    For the dancer wore a dazzling yellow dress for the first showing of musical Rock of Ages in Hollywood tonight.

    The former Dancing With The Stars favourite looked stunning in the elegant skin tight gown.

    The big screen adaptation, which focuses on the Eighties glam metal period, is just the 23-year-old's third stab at big screen success, following her turns in Burlesque and Footloose.

    Perky: The dancing champion looked excited to be on the red carpet surrounded by hundreds of fans

    Dancer's derriere: The daring outfit highlighted Julianne's impressive physique earned after years of practice

    Generations collide: It was a case of new Hollywood meeting old as she greeted fellow star Tom Cruise

    Queen of the night: Julianne posed with Catherine Zeta-Jones as Ryan Seacrest showed support for his lady

    And she will be hoping the silver screen version of the hit Broadway show performs better than those underwhelming motion pictures.

    The multi-talented star, who plays glam metal loving waitress Sherrie Christian in the film, will surely be hoping the stellar cast can help draw in the punters.

    Trying to steal the show: Stunning actress Malin Akerman donned this racy dress for her appearance

    Locking lips: Malin recently said her onscreen kiss with Tom was the 'tongueist tongue kiss of all time'

    Chatterboxes: A-list elite Catherine and Tom looked deep in conversation on the red carpet

    And she looked delighted when she caught up with the film's most famous performer Tom Cruise on the red carpet.

    The pair held hands and giggled as they basked in the excitement of the $80m picture's premiere.

    Paying tribute: Tom waved the Ronnie James Dio salute at the adoring crowd of fans

    Not so smart but casual: The Far and Away favourite decided to dress down for the fun premiere

    Sign of the times: Tom was attracting the attention of more fans than the hair metal star in attendance

    Tom, who plays aging rock frontman Stacee Jaxx near the end of the infamous 80s hair metal period,  even tried to win some heavy metal credibility by flashing the devil horn salute to his adoring fans.

    He also decided to dress down for the occasion, wearing a suede jacket, jeans, pullover and walking boots instead of the tried and trusted suited look.

    Dressed to impress: No one would deny the former Darling Buds of May star looked great for 42

    Showing how it should be done: Diego's suit looked far smarter than Tom's jeans and jacket combo

    The Far and Away favourite revelled in the rock star treatment he received from his excited fanbase, and gladly signed autographs for the crowd.

    While his back was turned Julianne sauntered off to grab a hold of her fellow leading star Diego Boneta, who plays assistant barman Drew Boley, who dreams of being a rock star.

    Tom's angels: The Mission Impossible star looked in his element as he posed with Catherine Zeta-Jones, Mary J. Blige and Julianne

    Slinky! Mary J Blije turned heads in her PVC and chiffon creation

    The pair, who have grown so close they even holiday together, looked at ease as they posed arm-in-arm for photographers.

    Catherine Zeta Jones struck a blow for more mature women everywhere by wearing a stunning skin tight sheer dress.

    Another exciting date night: Ensemble member Alec Baldwin brought along his fiance Hilaria Thomas for the night

    Getting in the spirit of things: Actor Josh Duhamel came out the hair metal closet as he attended with his wife Fergie, who wore a Cavalli dress

    In a Tis: High School Musical star Zac Efron seemed excited to meet Ashley Tisdale at the event

    Always on the job: Russell seems to be telling Alec a joke

    The 42-year-old Darling Buds of May star, who plays the movie's main villain, seemed to be having a ball as she flaunted her curves.

    It seems the story, which follows the fate of closure-threatened Sunset Strip waterhole The Bourbon Room, was too irresistible for Hollywood stars to resist.

    Adding a touch of cool: Def Leppard provide music for the soundtrack

    Never too young to rock: Poison thrust their rock horns for the camera

    For a host of other big names were also there, including Jennifer Aniston's beau Justin Theroux, who wrote the script, camp comic Russell Brand, who plays the club's manager, and Julianne's dancer brother Derek Hough.

    Josh Duhamel came out of the glam closet after he turned up in fancy dress with wife Fergie, while High School Musical star Zac Efron and Swedish Canadian actress Malin Akerman were also there.

    But a premiere celebrating the hair metal era would not be complete without a member of the genre's royalty, and Bret Michaels, who fronted arguable the style's most dubious outfit Poison, lapped up the chance at experiencing what it is like to be a star again.

    Justin time: Theroux looked excited to see his screenplay brought to life on the big screen

    Camp: Comic Russell Brand looked typically flamboyant while Julianne's brother Derek showed his support

    Pass the hairspray: Tom seemed excited to be posing with Bret Michaels, leader of infamously poppy hair metal outfit Posion

    Bret would be impressed: Tom stars as Arsenal singer Stacee Jaxx in the film adaptation of the musical hit

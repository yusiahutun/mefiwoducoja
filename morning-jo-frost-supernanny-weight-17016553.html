    Remember Supernanny Jo Frost? Wait until you see her insane weight loss

    29 Jun 2024, 00:00

    The Supernanny star made her return to TV on today’s episode of This Morning, leading a new parenting segment.

    But it wasn’t just her words of wisdom that impressed viewers – it was her svelte physique.

    The brunette looked to have shifted a whole lotta weight as she posed for pics with Holly and Phil in jeans and a curve-skimming black top.

    She accessorised with a printed scarf, leopard-print flats and a silver watch.

    Of course her trademark black glasses and arched eyebrows also made an appearance, alongside a nude pout and sweep of bronzer.

    On the show, the 45-year-old pressed the importance of a night-time routine for babies and toddlers.

    Her method, "Book, bath, bed," was well received by parents watching at home, who took to social media to sing her praises.

    "Thanks so much for speaking sense, Jo, don’t know what I’d do without you," one mum gushed.

    Another added: "My little one only wakes once per night since switching to this – works like a charm."

    Back in 2013, Jo confessed that she’d love to lose weight after struggling to find flattering clothes.

    "I’m a larger lady, a plus size with a bit of belly fat," she told The Guardian.

    "I’d like to reduce my weight, so I would feel healthier and look better in my clothes, but I’m not obsessed with scales and pounds."

    After rising to fame on Supernanny back in 2014, Jo jetted off to America and Europe to launch international versions of the show.

    While she tends to remain quiet about her personal life, she revealed on Loose Women last year that she was swamped with wedding planning.

    She also opened up about whether she would consider having her own children.

    "I have to say for families that foster, for families that adopt, for the professional nannies out there that are as integral and dedicated as myself in my profession with what I was doing, you don’t biologically need to have a child to feel love and to connect with children," she said.

    Aston Villa star Alisha Lehmann has been offered a $100k (£83.7k) package to join an adult subscription site.

    Lehmann, 24, has built up a mega social media following of 12million on Instagram thanks to her uploads in between starring in the Women's Super League and for the Switzerland national team. She regularly posts about her activities on and off the pitch.

    Subscription platform My.Club want her to cash in on her popularity by starting her own account with them. As an incentive, they've offered a $50k (£41.8k) signing-on fee and a further $50k donation to the Swiss women’s top flight.

    In an open letter to Lehmann, vice president Mike Ford wrote: "Given your growing legion of fans, which will only increase as the World Cup approaches this summer, My.Club would like to extend an offer to you to create an account and join the site.

    "It totally makes sense at this level of popularity to launch your ‘own club’. You can post training photos, thirst traps and personally field questions from fans and not have to deal with people sliding in your DMs 24/7."

    Lehmann's recent Insta upload, which featured seven snaps of her wearing a grey tracksuit, had her followers hailing her as "perfect" and "from another world".

    But in a recent interview with talkSPORT, she said the "nice" attention was secondary to her primary focus - which is very much her footy career.

    She said: “It’s more that it’s really nice, the support and everything and it’s also a big opportunity. I would like to show the world that women can actually do it in football and you don’t need to just to be normal.

    “You can also be a bit crazy, just be you, and use your personality to show the world who you are. Some people just see Instagram and social media and don’t even know I actually play football.

    “When I don’t post a football picture for a week, people say, ‘oh she doesn’t even play’. I’m a proper footballer. I work hard every day. I want to be the best version of myself in football and that’s my first priority in life.”

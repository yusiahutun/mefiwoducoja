    Add one more outfit to the list of Jenna Dewan's maternity fashion greatest hits. On Tuesday night, the actress and dancer attended the 2024 Fashion Trust U.S. Awards in Beverly Hills with her fiancé, Steve Kazee. For the occasion, she opted for a black Cult Gaia gown that included a bikini-style triangle bodice with a skirt attached at the center, creating two huge side cutouts.

    The swooping cutouts created a chance for her to show off her growing bump, which she highlighted with open trench coats and chic pajama dressing back in February. She and Kazee announced her third pregnancy in January. Dewan and Kazee share a 4-year-old son named Callum. She also shares a 10-year-old daughter, Everly, whom she shares with her ex-husband, Channing Tatum. 

    Dewan accessorized her feathered look with jewels from Suzanne Kalan and Cicada, including shimmering diamond drop earrings with a botanical leaf design and rings on almost every finger. Her dark brown hair was parted deep on one side. For his part, Kazee wore a relaxed, slouchy chocolate-brown suit with a ribbed tank top underneath. 

    Dewan has worn Cult Gaia in the past. Back in November 2023, she attended the brand's Resort 2024 presentation in Los Angeles, according to WWD. 

    “I’ve just entered the second trimester, and I feel alive again,” Dewan said of her pregnancy during a January 2024 interview with Romper, adding that she will continue to work on The Rookie throughout her pregnancy. “[Working] helps balance my mind and The Rookie is truly the most dreamy, incredible job. Everyone is so lovely. It’s really fun, and it’s easy, and it flows, and so I think that’s a big part of why it’s easy for me to work pregnant.”

    Fashion Trust U.S. is a non-profit organization that works to support emerging designers. Its annual awards ceremony honors individuals working in various categories, including jewelry, ready-to-wear, and accessories. Fashion Trust also presents awards for inclusivity and sustainability. This year's winners included MoAnA Luu of ManLuu, Ashley Harris of Don’t Let Disco, Batsheva Hay, Yamil Arbaje, Esha Soni, and Harbison Studio. The Fashion Trust U.S. winners received grants of between $100,000 and $200,000, as well as mentoring.

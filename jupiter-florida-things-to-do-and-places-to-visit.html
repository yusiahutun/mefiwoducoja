    Fun Fact: Jupiter Florida state ranked 12th among the best beach towns in the US in 2018 and the 9th happiest seaside town in the US in 2021. The beach town of Jupiter has also been on the best beach towns to live in lists of several respected publications and forums. And rightfully so, from monuments of historical significance to fun events, Jupiter has got it all. No wonder that Jupiter is considered one of the most lavish areas in the US and is home to stars like Tiger Woods and Michael Jordan.

    Fortunately, according to the Jupiter radar on accurate weather forecasting websites would tell you that ​​the fall season transitioning into winter is the best time to visit Jupiter. As the heat of the summer starts to dissipate, locals start emerging to take advantage of Florida’s bounty of natural beauty. 

    From surfing to golfing, and exotic fruits to al fresco food festivals, Jupiter has a lot to offer its visitors. Here’s a look at the top places to visit and things to do in Jupiter Florida.

    Places to Visit in Jupiter Florida During Winters:

    1. Busch Wildlife Sanctuary

    The Busch Wildlife Sanctuary can easily make you feel like you’re in the wild with a path leading through swamps, Flatwoods, and oak hammocks. Along the way, visitors can check out some of Florida’s best wildlife, including pelicans, owls, and otters, who live in spacious cages in this outdoor sanctuary. 

    2. Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse & Museum

    The Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse is one of only three of its kind in the country. This natural area boasts 120 acres of nature preserve located at the confluence of the Indian and Loxahatchee rivers. Visitors can hike the trail along the north side of the preserve to the observation tower. From the tower, visitors can enjoy a looming view of the lagoon and spot manatees in their natural habitat.

    3. Dubois Park

    Dubois Park is located at the mouth of Jupiter Inlet, right across from the southern tip of the swanky Jupiter Island. Visitors can lounge on the sandy beaches lined by palm trees and enjoy the views of the Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse. Tourists can enjoy swimming, picnic, fishing, kayaking, and more activities in this park. The Dubois Park is also home to the Dubois Pioneer Home, which is one of the last historic homesteads in Jupiter.

    4. Roger Dean Stadium

    The Roger Dean Chevrolet Stadium is a must-visit for baseball fans. This stadium is the sprint-training base for the Miami Marlins and St. Louis Cardinals. This epic stadium is also home to Jupiter Hammerheads and three other minor league teams throughout the year. Even if you’re not big on baseball, you’ll enjoy visiting the stadium and catching a game.

    5. Jonathan Dickinson State Park

    The Jonathan Dickinson State Park is an 11,500-acre natural wonderland, which is open to tourists for a small fee. The park that served as a World War II army base for secret radar training is now an expansive park offering water and land activities. Visitors can enjoy buggy rides, trail rides, boat tours and rent motorboats, bikes, kayaks, or canoes.

    Things to Do In Jupiter Florida During Winters

    1. Go Shark Diving

    Florida is home to more shark species than any other place in the US. There are companies offering shark diving charters out of Jupiter. Visitors can use this service to get up close and personal with sharks, including the Black Tip Shark, which is in the area only during the annual fall migration. 

    2. Play Golf

    The city has been named the Golf-Pro Capital of the World by Golf.com. This is because around 35 professional golfers are living here all year round. Some of the famous names include Tiger Woods, Jack Nicklaus, Justin Thomas, and Dustin Johnson. The PGA season begins to gain momentum in the fall, which brings more pros to the area to compete and practice. Head to Jupiter Dunes or Abacoa Golf Club to spot and even practice with one of these pros.

    3. Eat Exotic Produce

    Florida has perfect subtropical weather to grow exotic fruits, which can be picked fresh by appointment at Jupiter farms. From September to December, visitors can pick passion fruit, longan, and mamey sapote. You can also buy seasonal exotic fruits from Jupiter’s Farmer’s Market.

    4. Experience Fright Nights

    October is the spooky season, and Jupiter Florida is home to one of the scariest adventures in the state. The Fright Nights haunted adventure is hosted across the South Florida Fairgrounds in West Palm Beach. The event has multiple sections with different storylines portrayed by actors.

    These were the top things to do and places to visit in Jupiter Florida. However, this is just a glimpse of what the place has to offer. If you are on vacation in the US, this place should be near the top of your list.

    Early life

    Alan Jackson was born Alan Eugene Jackson on 17 October 1958 in Georgia, United States. He is the son of Joseph Eugene Jackson and Ruth Musick Jackson. He is the youngest of five siblings: Mattie Denise Jackson, Dani Grace Jackson, and Alexandra Jane Jackson.

    Alan grew up in Newman, Georgia. He attended Elm Street Elementary and later joined Newnan High School. But after graduating, he did not go to college. Instead, he joined the band Dixie Steel. When he turned 27, he moved from Georgia to Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue music full-time.

    Professional career

    The country music singer listened to gospel music while growing up. After high school, he joined a local band called Dixie Steel. However, he waited until 27 to move from Georgia to Tennessee to pursue music full-time.

    He released his pre-debut demo album in 1987. The album put him on the map as a talented songwriter. However, he only released the album in Japan. In 1989, Jackson became the first artist signed to the country division of Arista Records and released his debut album, Here in the Real World.

    Where Is 'Blue Bloods' Actually Filmed?

    Blue Bloods, CBS's long-running fictional police drama, is coming back this fall for its ninth season. The premiere doesn't air until Friday, September 28, but cast and crew have already been spotted filming new episodes.

    18 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Blue Bloods, CBS's long-running fictional police drama, is coming back this fall for its ninth season. The premiere doesn't air until Friday, September 28, but cast and crew have already been spotted filming new episodes.

    The show, centered around a family of New York Police Department officers, obviously takes place in New York City. Police Commissioner Frank Reagan was born and raised in the Bay Ridge neighborhood of Brooklyn. He raised a generation of cops and lawyers in the Reagan household, and still resides there today. (Where else would he host those famous family dinners on Sundays?!) Many of the other scenes, however, occur throughout the boroughs, as the Reagan family fights crime in different areas of the Big Apple. But are these fictional scenarios actually captured in the city they're set in?

    Unlike many scripted television series, Blue Bloods is filmed where the story takes place. Recently, the CBS All Access Twitter account shared the real-life locations for a number of shows on the network, from Blue Bloods to NCIS.

    "There's certainly a Brooklyn vibe in [Blue Bloods], which makes sense considering the show's stages and offices are all in the borough," the tweet reads. "Of course, being there means it's convenient to shoot on-location around New York City, be it Queens or the East Village."

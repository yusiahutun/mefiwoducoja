    Giant telecommunications company Safaricom has announced the closure of all its shops in Nairobi from Friday, October 6 to Saturday, October 7.

    In a notice posted on its social media pages, the telco, however, noted its Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) shop would remain open during the period.

    Safaricom explained that the closure would enable its staff to gather for an internal staff event.

    "Kindly note that all shops in Nairobi, excluding JKIA, will remain closed on Friday, October 6. Normal services will resume on Saturday, October 7.

    What's Safaricom's deal with Aliexpress?

    Earlier, TUKO.co.ke reported that Safaricom signed a partnership deal with an e-commerce global trading company, Aliexpress.com, that will see customers buy and pay for their goods online through M-Pesa.

    Aliexpress is part of the Chinese e-commerce giant Alibaba, whose founder and chief executive Jack Ma was in the country to incorporate African vendors and shoppers into the global value chain.

    The telecommunication giant signed the deal to bolster M-Pesa's position in the country's mobile payment sector.

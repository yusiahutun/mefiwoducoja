    Claudia Schiffer knew exactly what she was doing when she walked down the aisle in May 2002 and married the man of her dreams, Matthew Vaughn. Since that day, the producer and the German model have been accomplishing their goals with each other by their side and they wouldn’t want to have it any other way.

    “I’d never found anyone that perfect and I just thought, oh, there’s no perfect man out there. Then I met him,” Claudia gushed about her hubby during a previous interview with The Guardian. “He had everything I wanted in a man. He completely swept me off my feet.”

    Matthew knows his wife so well. When he proposed to her, the film producer bought her a tortoise instead of an engagement ring, which Claudia absolutely loved. “We are so similar in many ways. We got on like a house on fire from the minute we met. It was really natural,” she said.

    The fashionista and Matthew share their two daughters, Clementine and Cosima, and their only son, Caspar. Before they welcomed Caspar, their oldest child, in January 2003, Claudia told her hubby she was going to change her lifestyle for her family. “I used to work every single day and travel around the world. I worked weekends and I never took one second off,” she previously told Vogue. “When I met my husband I said, ‘You know what, this is important. I’m not going to work weekends any more’ — and when I had kids, I became even more careful.”

    Like Claudia, Matthew has been trying his best to balance his family and work life. As a well-known director in Hollywood, he’s been producing great films like 2010’s Kick-Ass and 2014’s Kingman: The Secret Service. But when he’s not on the set of his next big movie, you can find him hanging out with Claudia and their children at their beautiful England home.

    R&B is a preferred genre of music for lots of people. The best R&B albums of all time are relaxing, and most of the songs are easy to sing along to. Several artists who have ventured into this genre are now household names. And from the look of things, they have more in store for their fans.

    R&B is a timeless class of music. Every era has had icons who have gained popularity and sold millions of albums. The best R&B albums of all time span over a long period of time. Some have remained popular over time, providing sufficient entertainment to those who listen to them.

    Best R&B albums of all time

    There are several ways to ascertain the best R&B albums. One of them is the number of copies sold, and the awards garnered. Another way is by looking up their download and viewership volumes. The more, the better. Here are some of the best albums of all time:

    1. Beyoncé Knowles - Homecoming: The Live Album

    This is Queen Bee's fifth album. The four-disc vinyl was released in 2019. It contains 40 tracks, which feature Solange, Jay-Z, Kelly Rowland, and Michelle Williams. Homecoming has bagged two awards so far: Grammy Award for Best Music Film in 2020, and NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Variety – Series or Special, in 2019.

    2. Solange - A Seat at the Table

    This 2016 release made it to number 1 on the Billboard charts in October of that year. Four songs off the album also made it to the top 20 singles on Billboard's Hot R&B Songs Chart. This project warmed the hearts of many African Americans because it speaks about what it means to be black in America.

    Solange Knowles, who is Beyonce's sister, also bagged a Grammy Award for Best R&B Performance in the year 2016 for the song "Cranes in the sky,'' which is also off the collection. The 16-track album sold 72,000 units by the end of 2016.

    3. Beyonce - Lemonade

    Lemonade is Beyonce's sixth studio album. A short film follows it. It was released in April 2016 and sold over 1.5 million copies in the United States by the end of that year. It features, among others, Jay-Z, Zendaya, Serena Williams and Halle Bailey.

    The musical has won several awards, namely:

    • 2017 Grammy Award for best progressive R&B album
    • 2017 NAACP Image Award for outstanding album
    • 2016 soul train music Award for album/mixtape of the year
    • Billboard Music Award for Top R&B album in 2017
    • BET Award for album of the year in 2017

    4. Frank Ocean - Channel Orange

    Channel Orange was Frank Ocean's debut studio album. It was released in July 2012 by Def Jam Recordings and has 16 tracks. The hit song ''Thinking 'Bout You'', which was off this album has over 5 million views on YouTube.

    The album has garnered awards including:

    • Grammy Award for best progressive R&B album in 2013
    • Soul Train Music Award for album/mixtape of the year in 2012
    • GLAAD Media Award for outstanding music artist in 2013.

    5. Janelle Monae - The Archandroid

    This album was released in May 2010. It was Janelle's first studio album, following an EP she had released in 2007 dubbed ''Metropolis: The Chase Suite''. It has 18 tracks, which are mostly R&B, but also include funk, rap and disco.

    The album showcases Janelle's musical prowess and vocal range. This collection also brought her to the limelight and had a whooping first-week sale of 21,000 copies. It was also nominated for the Grammy Awards Best Contemporary R&B album in the year 2010.

    6. Jill Scott - Who is Jill Scott? Words and Sounds Vol. 1

    The 18-track project was released in July 2000. This is Jill's debut collection and features a bit of soul, hip-hop and jazz. The intro to the album is a poem, which shows off her skill in poetry as well. ''A Long Walk,'' which is off this album, boasts over 20 million YouTube views.

    This musical powerhouse was nominated to the Grammys for the best R&B album in 2001, and the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Album in the same year. She won the Soul Train Music Award for R&B/Soul Album – Female category in 2001. She has sold over 2 million copies.

    7. Alicia Keys - The Diary of Alicia Keys

    ''The Diary'' is also one of the top R&B albums of all time. It contains 15 tracks, most of which were instant hits after its release in 2003. Alicia won the Grammy award for best R&B album in 2005, and the Soul Train Music Award for Best R&B/Soul Album – Female category in 2005.

    Alicia Keys sold over 600,000 copies of ''The Diary'' in its first week of release. So far, the album has sold over 20 million copies, making it one of the top selling R&B albums of all time. The album has also won several top sales awards.

    8. Usher - Confessions

    Usher Raymond is a musical icon who gained popularity in the early 2000s. 'Confessions' is his fourth album, containing 21 tracks. It was released in March 2004 and contains several hit songs, including ''Caught up'' and 'Yeah.' The album has won several awards, including:

    • Billboard Music Award for Top R&B Album in 2004
    • Billboard Music Award for Top Billboard 200 Album in 2004
    • MTV Europe Music Award for Best Album in 2004
    • American Music Award for Favorite Album Soul/R&B in 2004
    • American Music Award for Favorite Album Pop/Rock in 2004
    • Grammy Award for Best Contemporary R&B Album in 2005
    • Soul Train Music Award for Best R&B/Soul Album – Male category in 2005

    9. Luther Vandross - Dance with my Father

    This is one of the classic R&B albums of all time. It was released in 2003 and has 14 tracks. The single ''Dance with my Father,'' which greatly contributed to the success of this album, has over 100 million views on YouTube.

    In its first year, it sold 1.5 million copies. The album has several awards to its name, including American Music Award for Favorite Album Soul/R&B in 2003, Grammy Award for Best R&B Album in 2004, and NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Album in 2004. It also has several nominations to its name.

    10. Joe Thomas - My name is Joe

    Joe Thomas released this 14-track collection in Apil 2000. Some of the popular tracks on it are, ''I Believe in You.'' and ''Treat Her Like a Lady.'' My name is Joe is his third studio album, and also his bestseller, having sold over 3 million copies in the United States.

    He was nominated for the Best R&B album at the 2001 Grammies, and also for the NAACP Image Award for Outstanding album in the same year. He also landed at position three at the Billboard Top Hot 100 artists, male category, and number 5 for the top R&B/Hip hop artists.

    11. Aaliyah - Aaliyah

    The late Aaliyah released this self-titled album in July 2001, before she passed away in August of the same year. It was her third studio collection and contains 15 tracks. Some people referred to her as the princess of R & B. Before her death, the album had made sales of almost half a million copies.

    The album received several nominations, from the Grammies to the Soul Train music awards, and the NAACP Image Awards. In 2002, the American Music Awards named her the Favourite soul/R&B female artist. The Billboard Music Awards honoured her as Top Hip-Hop/R&B Artist of the Year, Female category, in the years 2000 and 2003.

    12. Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill

    Lauryn, who was a part of The Fugees, released this solo R&B collection in 1998. It was a different sound from the hip hop one that her fans had been used to. It also has some songs done in reggae and hip-hop, and contains 16 tracks.

    • Billboard Music Award for Top R&B Album in 1998

    • American Music Award for Favorite Album Soul/R&B in 2000

    13. Boyz II Men - 11

    This American quartet has also produced one of the greatest R&B albums of all time. The 13-track collection features popular songs like ''I'll Make Love to You'' and ''On Bended Knee.'' It was released in 1994 and has sold over 10 million copies.

    14. Tevin Campbell - I'm Ready

    I'm Ready is a 12-track album which was released in 1993, when Tevin was only 16 years old. This is his second studio album and has sold over 2 million copies. The title track, ''I'm Ready'' has over 13 million views on YouTube.

    The single was also nominated for the Best Male R&B Vocal Performance at the 1994 Grammy Awards. The album was also nominated for Best R & B album in the same year. ''Can we talk,'' which is also off the album, was on the Billboard R & B chart for three weeks.

    15. Mariah Carey - Mariah Carey

    Mariah is ranked as the best selling female artist of the US. This album sold over 4 million copies in the United States. The album was nominated for the Grammy Award Album of the year in 1990, among many others in various categories.

    Joel Embiid’s biography

    The NBA basketball player was born in Yaoundé, Cameroon on the 16th of March, 1994. He is the son of a military officer by the name of Thomas (father) and his wife Christine. He had two younger siblings, a brother known as Arthur and a sister. His younger brother died in a car crash in 2014.

    Embiid grew up playing different sports such as volleyball and soccer. His original plans were to be a professional volleyball player in Europe. When he was 15 years old he started playing basketball after being influenced by NBA Hall of Fame centre Hakeem Olajuwon.

    Joel was discovered at a basketball camp by Luc Mbah a Moute who helped him relocate to the United States. The education journey of Embiid in the United States started at Montverde Academy. After just one year, he transferred to The Rock School where he could get more playing time.

    Career beginnings

    When Embiid was about to finish high school he committed to the University of Kansas where he was a five-star recruit. He only attended the university for one year but managed to be named among the 30 finalists for the Naismith College Player of the Year. His college career averaged 11.2 points, 8.1 rebounds, 1.4 assists and 2.6 blocks.

    He was drafted into the NBA in 2014 by the Philadelphia 76ers. He suffered a broken navicular bone in his foot and was ruled unlikely to play at all in the 2014-15 season. A week later he was selected with the third overall pick by the Philadelphia 76ers making him the third Cameroonian-born NBA player.

    Rookie player

    2016-17 was Joel's rookie season as he started at centre in the 76ers’ first preseason game against the Boston Celtics. His long-awaited NBA regular-season debut came on October 26, 2016, against the Oklahoma City Thunder. He registered his first career double-double with 18 points and 10 rebounds against the Orlando Magic.

    On November 19 he scored a career-high 26 points in just 20 minutes to help the 76ers beat the Phoenix Suns 120-105.     In 2018 he was named a starter in the NBA All-Star Game. He continued producing spectacular displays frequently being named a player of the week. His game has been disrupted by injuries and the two-game suspension following an altercation with Karl-Anthony Towns.

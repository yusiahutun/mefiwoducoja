    Man arrested over cutting brake line on girlfriend's car after furious row

    By Wil Longbottom for MailOnline
    Updated: 03:38 EST, 3 January 2012

    View
    comments


    Row: Kevin Reilly, 36, has been charged with cutting his girlfriend's brake lines in Woodbridge, Virginia

    A 36-year-old man has been charged with cutting the brake lines on his girlfriend's car to prevent her from walking out on him.

    Kevin Reilly, of Woodbridge, Virginia, has been charged with two counts of attempted malicious wounding, vehicle tampering and destruction of property.

    After a furious row with Reilly his 23-year-old girlfriend - who has not been named - got into the car with her two-year-old son and attempted to leave.

    She noticed she was having difficulty stopping the car, so took it to a repair shop and they informed her the brake line had been cut.

    Investigators later determined her boyfriend, Reilly, had cut the line.

    Jonathan Perok, Prince William County Police spokesman, said: 'The victim reported to us that she and her two-year-old child got into a vehicle and were attempting to leave.

    'She noticed there was an issue with her brakes.

    'She had a previous domestic encounter with [Reilly] over an ongoing issue, and it was determined he was the one who cut the brake line.'

    Danger: Reilly's unnamed girlfriend noticed she was having difficulty stopping after walking out on her boyfriend and getting in her car with her 2-year-old son

    Brooke Lockamy, Reilly's sister, said her sibling was only trying to prevent his girlfriend from leaving.

    She told Fox: 'He was afraid she was going to leave. He didn't sever the brake line, he pulled it loose.

    'His intention was to make sure she would not leave by disabling the car.'

    How Does UV-C Light Kill Germs?

    UV-C light destroys the DNA and RNA in bacteria, removing their ability to multiply and cause disease. While it hasn’t been proven to kill the Covid-19 virus, it provides a chemical-free method to killing mold, mildew, and other viruses.

    UV-C light has been used for killing germs in places like hospitals, airplanes, and water treatment plants. And thanks to technology, that same UV-C light comes in the form of household devices such as sanitizing bags, cell phone cases, water bottles, and light bulbs.

    Larger-coverage UV-C lights — such as the stand-alone device and light bulbs below — use a wavelength that creates ozone gases. These provide more thorough germicidal coverage, but also have a strong odor and should be used away from people, pets, and plants.

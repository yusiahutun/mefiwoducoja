    24 Sep 2024, 00:00

    You needn’t have a TikTok account to notice that bombshell curls, the viral hairstyle of 2022, has successfully jumped off the platform into real life. Endless video tutorials demonstrate methods for achieving big bouncy volume with your hair, from the old school (which has resulted in the resurgence of traditional hair rollers), to the more advanced (causing the coveted Dyson Airwrap to sell out time and again).

    All achieve – or attempt to achieve – the look synonymous with Nineties supermodel icons and the Seventies Studio 54 party scene. Think sky-high root lift and big body throughout the hair, with loose spiral curls and fresh-from-the-salon bounce. And yes, it’s possible on most hair types, provided you have the length.

    But if you’re wondering what styling methods the professionals recommended for the 'do – and which is best for your hair’s health – you’ve come to the right place. Here, two of London’s leading hairstylists weigh in on the trending topic.

    “Whether on the red carpet or in the salon, I have seen a rise in requests for glam, full-bodied hair recently,” says BaByliss ambassador and celebrity go-to Syd Hayes. He favours the use of heated rollers to achieve the look.

    “Heated rollers create volume, bounce and a large curl to the hair and [are] always a session stylist’s secret trick for adding instant volume.” He rates the BaByliss Thermo-Ceramic Hair Rollers, a set of 20 heated rollers combining small, medium and large sizes.

    BaByliss Thermo-Ceramic Hair Rollers - Black

    BaByliss Thermo-Ceramic Hair Rollers - Black

    How to use heated rollers for big bouncy curls

    As with any styling tool intended to achieve curls, size matters. “The smaller the roller the tighter the bounce/curl, so think carefully about the look you’re looking to achieve,” Hayes advises. For this classic look, try Hayes’ top tips:

    – "Starting on the top sections will ensure these are left in the longest, giving more height on the top."

    – "Use a light hairspray on each section before you wrap the hair around the roller, starting from the end and making sure it is wrapped in neatly, as this will create a nice curl right to the tips of the hair."

    – "You can use a clip or pin to secure the roller to the head, the tighter the better."

    – "The longer you leave the roller in, the longer the shape and bounce will last. Let the rollers cool down to set the shape before taking them out."

    Hot versus cool styling

    If it’s a look you want to achieve regularly, be mindful of the potential damage that continued heat styling can have on your hair. A-list favourite, founder of eponymous salons, and Dyson’s global hair ambassador, Larry King, says, “I would stay away from heated rollers because they really do cause damage, especially to the lengths and ends of the hair”. Instead, he advocates styling without high heat, with the use of Dyson’s innovative Airwrap, a styler harnessing aerodynamics which curves air to attract and wrap hair to its barrel.

    Dyson HS01 Airwrap Complete Hair Styler

    Dyson HS01 Airwrap Complete Hair Styler

    “The Airwrap comes with so many different attachments, from the smoothing brush to the various barrel sizes, so you can create different looks all the time,” King notes, which also means it can be used to achieve this trend on diverse hair types. “You can easily brush out natural texture first using it,” he explains. “So, say if you have 'type 3' curls and want to achieve the smooth, big bouncy volume, you can stretch out the natural curl first using the smoothing brush attachment, and then work through the hair with the barrel attachment to put big curls back in.” If you were to use rollers on this hair type you wouldn’t achieve that luscious blow-dry look, he says “– you’d get more of a frizzier result”.

    When it comes to maintaining the condition of your hair without heat – which the Airwrap is known and loved for – TikTok has served up all kinds of tutorials for alternative heatless curls, including the use of silk curling rods and similar DIY versions (using t-shirts, tights, and even socks, depending on your hair type). Then, of course there’s the humble self-grip Velcro rollers.

    “If heat damage is a concern, Velcro rollers will help maintain the condition of the hair. But you might have to do other work initially before you put them in to smooth out the hair – which might mean that you’re using intensive heat which can cause damage in a similar way as hot styling tools,” King notes.

    Also, he adds that you will achieve one key look with Velcro rollers, “which is these big and bouncy curls”. Whereas an investment in the Airwrap buys you endless options, “from doing big and bouncy, to marcel waves, beach waves, flicked-out the ends, straight with the smoothing brush,” and more. No doubt those TikTokers who’ve parted with £449 for the tool – galvanising a sell-out – are busy demonstrating these other looks, too.

    How to use the Airwrap for big bouncy curls

    "To create a big and bouncy pin set curl it’s so easy," King says. Try his top tips:

    – "Take a section and let it wrap the hair around the barrel, ensuring you roll it up to the root–so you get loads of root lift."

    – "Use the cool shot for a couple of seconds to set the shape, then slide the hair out of the barrel holding the curl in place and then pin it to the head using a section clip."

    – "Do this until you’ve worked around the whole head."

    The best products for big bouncy curls

    Whatever styling tools you're using, Hayes says to “make sure the hair is first washed and dried with a volumising product.” Here, King recommends “a volumising hair mist for soft, touchable volume and a strong mouse for long-lasting hold”.

    Decoding the intimate importance of the jewellery box

    When our loved ones pass away, there comes the inevitably poignant task of sifting through their belongings, sorting this for charity, that for a memory box, the rest to be discarded. There is one item, however, that feels exceptionally intimate to sort through though: a jewellery box. I have emptied knicker drawers that have felt less intimate than thumbing through the two jewellery boxes that I have inspected, those of my late mother-in-law and my Nanna. Jewellery boxes tell us so much about an individual - from the well-worn but worthless trinkets prized over more expensive, barely-worn baubles, to the charms and talismans chosen to celebrate birthdays, weddings, births and deaths - sometimes accompanied by love letters, locks of hair, ribbons from bouquets or even baby teeth.

    With both of these women, I could see their life stories and personalities played out in the jewels and their containers; my mother-in-law - a born magpie and peacock with a love of bold sapphires, glittering diamonds and opals, all housed in a gleaming wooden box with several tiers. My Nanna, far less interested in precious things, had a simple string of costume pearls, silver charms bearing Catholic saints, and a cut-glass pendant backed with iridescent foil (complete with a sizeable chip because I played with it as a child) all tucked into an unassuming, brown felt-covered case.

    It seems fitting that at a time when we are cherishing the small pleasures in life and the preciousness of our loved ones, that jewellery designers should now be focusing not only on the content of our jewellery boxes, but the boxes themselves -which can be just as beautiful and individualistic as their treasured contents.

    Jessica McCormack

    Jessica McCormack launched her bespoke jewellery box commissioning service in 2018, and a new collaboration launched today in partnership with the Haas brothers - an LA design duo known for their whimsical motifs - has taken jewellery box crafting to another level. Along with an exclusive collection of quirky jewels, the jewellery box looks as if it has sauntered straight out of the daydreams of Lewis Carroll, a richly hued coromandel box set upon bronze legs, filled with colourful embroidered illustrations by Niki Haas. "The embroidery brings Niki’s fantastical illustrations to life, which are full of childhood nostalgia - and then setting it upon these beautiful, magical, animalistic legs makes it so much more than just a box to house treasures in - it’s a treasure on its own," explains McCormack.

    Jessica McCormack

    Describing the box as almost like a living, breathing friend, McCormack has created a home for precious things that feels as alive as the stories our jewels divulge. "I think what’s so nice about our jewellery boxes is that on opening them you enter a new world. It’s more than just functional, it's joyful and experiential," she adds. McCormack’s previous boxes have been embroidered with myriad illustrations and mottos, each crafted as a bespoke project for a client and each as equally precious as their contents. "The finished product is so personal to them, and there’s always so much emotion poured into the designs - the client and what they want remains our focus. Often they’re given as gifts – we feel lucky to be a part of something so sentimental and special,” she says.

    Mini jewellery box, £22

    Mini jewellery box, £22

    The role that jewellery boxes play in our lives is carefully considered by designers at both ends of the price spectrum. "Jewellery boxes shouldn’t just serve a practical function, they are as much a reflection of your personality as your collection of jewellery is," says Louise Bartlett, co-founder of jewellery and accessories brand Estella Bartlett, whose designs retail at around £22. “Our jewellery boxes are designed to match a range of personalities, from bold appliqué designs to delicate pastel colours, and we’ve put as much thought into each of them as we have with our jewellery,” she adds. Small and light enough to be transportable, yet with considered fabric choices and peppy mottos inscribed on top, they are a chic and petite solution for those who don’t need to stash away the entire Crown Jewels.

    Sophie Bille Brahe Velvet jewellery box

    Sophie Bille Brahe Velvet jewellery box

    Those looking for a middling ground will be seduced by Sophie Bille Brahe’s velvet jewellery box designs, who is seeing ever increasing demand for her hard case Tresor style. "There is now much more of a focus on really feeling proud of your belongings and taking good care of the things that you own in order to sustain their longevity," says Bille Brahe. "I wanted to create the perfect jewellery box to store my jewellery in when travelling. I often found that when I was packing for a trip I didn’t have a box that was the right size, felt secure and also looked really beautiful."

    Her boxes are the antidote to all of these problems, with the Tresor style frequently sold out on Matches Fashion and Net-a-Porter, snapped up for its luscious plush velvet in jewel tones, with a dainty tassel as a finishing flourish. "There has been a change in attitude over recent years and jewellery is something that people really treasure," Bille Brahe adds. "We have seen that when customers are purchasing a beautiful pair of earrings or a special necklace they also want to feel they have the perfect place to keep their jewellery." It is easy to imagine that when all of these precious boxes are eventually passed down, they will be as well-loved as the treasures they conceal.

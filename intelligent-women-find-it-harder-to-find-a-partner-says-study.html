    A growing number of women are freezing their eggs because they're unable to find equally educated partners.

    Yale University reports that fewer men are entering higher education, resulting in an "over-supply" of highly educated women who are struggling to find their romantic match. Essentially, there is a widening intelligence gap between men and women and it's preventing the latter from the meeting their male equals.

    According to The Independent, the US study examined 150 women in the US and Israel undergoing egg freezing at eight clinics. More than 90 per cent said they were "preserving" their eggs because they were single or unmarried and 81 percent of women interviewed in the study had a college degree.

    "There is a major gap - they are literally missing men," said author of the study Marcia Ihorn, a Professor of Anthropology at Yale. "There are not enough college graduates for them. In simple terms, this is about an oversupply of educated women."

    Furthermore, fertility director is quoted in the research as saying: "[Straight] women tell us frequently that they are freezing their eggs because the men they meet feel threatened by their success and so are unwilling to commit to starting a family together."

    Of course, this is no reason to play down intellect or achievements. After all, women are cleverer than all that.

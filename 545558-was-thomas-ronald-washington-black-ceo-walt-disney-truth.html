    The series does not include white characters to make the audience relate to the black characters as white characters would. It is this approach that the series creator introduced a character named Thomas Ronald Washington and labelled him as the first black CEO of The Walt Disney Company.

    The claim

    Thomas Ronald Washington is often cited as the first black CEO of The Walt Disney Company. The claim has been perpetuated through various social media platforms and blogs. However, a closer examination of Disney's history and leadership reveals a different story.

    Thomas Ronald Washington, portrayed as the first black CEO of The Walt Disney Company in the TV series Atlanta, is a fictional character in Atlanta created by Francesca Sloane and Joseph Adcock. Despite the compelling narrative presented in the show, Thomas Washington is not a real person, and his story is a work of fiction.

    Norman's career at Disney was not always smooth. In the early years, he faced racial discrimination and was often assigned to menial tasks. However, he persevered and eventually became a key animator, working on Disney movies like The Jungle Book and The Aristocats.

    His groundbreaking contributions to the animation industry paved the way for more diverse representation within Disney and influenced the hiring of other black animators.

    To date, the Walt Disney Company has not had a black CEO. The company has had several CEOs, including Walt Disney himself, Roy O. Disney, Card Walker, Ron Miller, Michael Eisner, Bob Iger, and Bob Chapek, but none of these individuals have been black.

    Diversity and inclusion at Disney

    While Disney does not have a black CEO, the company has tried to increase diversity and inclusion in its leadership ranks and overall workforce. In recent years, Disney has implemented various initiatives to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion.

    In 2020, Disney launched its Reimagine Tomorrow campaign, which focuses on increasing diversity, equity, and inclusion in its storytelling, workforce, and community engagement.

    The campaign includes programs like the Disney Launchpad incubator, which provides underrepresented filmmakers with opportunities to develop their projects. Disney has also made strides in increasing diversity in its leadership ranks.

    In 2020, the company announced the appointment of Latondra Newton as its Chief Diversity Officer. Newton is responsible for leading Disney's diversity, equity, and inclusion efforts across the company. However, she exited the company in 2023.

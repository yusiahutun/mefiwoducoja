    Dior's fashion shows never fail to make an impact. Over the weekend, the fashion house hosted its Cruise 2024 show in Mexico City, which paid tribute to the legendary artist Frida Kahlo. The spectacular show took place at the Antiguo Colegio de San Ildefonso, a museum and former school, where Kahlo studied and met her husband, Diego Rivera.

    “I first saw her work at an exhibition in Rome when I was a teenager, the first show I’d ever seen by a woman artist, and it made a lifelong impression,” creative director Maria Grazia Chiuri said backstage. “It really touched me deeply. Her work is so inspiring for women. What she did for her time was unbelievable.”

    WWD//Getty Images

    The show served as a love letter to Kahlo's work and feminist spirit, as well as an homage to Mexico, incorporating local craftspeople. Among the famous faces invited to Mexico City to watch the show was tennis sensation Emma Raducanu – wearing a look from Dior's pre-autumn/winter show, which was staged earlier this year in Mumbai – who was joined by the likes of Naomi Watts, Alicia Keys and Riley Keough.

    Mayra Carreño

    We caught up with Raducanu ahead of the show to chat all things Dior, why fashion matters and her style inspiration.

    Let's talk through your show outfit – why does this look in particular feel like a good fit for you?

    "I really love my Dior look for the show, you can really notice the craftsmanship and all the embroideries on the jacket created in collaboration with the Indian artisans. I spent some time in India myself playing tournaments – it's somewhere I've always loved going back to, as the people were so friendly – and I just thought there was such a vibrant energy about the place. I'm very happy to be wearing this tonight."

    Mayra Carreño

    Mayra Carreño

    Mayra Carreño

    How do you decide which brands you want to work with?

    "For me, it's really important when deciding which brands I would like to partner with that I have an organic connection to them and I identify with them. It's really important to me to stay true to who I am as a person, which means working with brands that align with my values."

    Mayra Carreño

    How involved do you tend to be with the styling process?

    "I get really involved with the styling process! I find it really fun, especially as an athlete as I'm typically in sports clothes most of the time. So on the occasions that I do get to dress up I like to express myself and when wearing Dior I always feel super empowered and confident. That's why I like to have a say myself in what I'm wearing – it's a really fun process."

    Mayra Carreño

    What kind of fashion makes you feel your most confident?

    "It's all about the material and quality. I personally notice the difference. I also like to wear more neutral tones with the occasional pop of colour or pattern, or accessorising it with jewellery. It's really fun to add in a twist."

    How has your style evolved over the years?

    "My style has remained rather similar throughout the years. I grew up with my mum dressing me and as I've grown older, I have just cultivated that and added my own twist to it occasionally. I was very lucky to have such a strong influence on my style and fashion from my mum.

    "I also really enjoy that nautical, marine type of style and those colours; navys, whites, blues. I love wearing all white. I take inspiration depending on a lot of places that I go to or the city that I'm in, but I've always loved pretty minimal styles and blues, that's my favourite colour."

    Mayra Carreño

    What has been your most memorable fashion moment in recent years and why?

    "My most memorable fashion moment so far has to be a shoot at the Ritz Hotel in London, where I wore a red Dior gown on some really incredible stairs and down their beautiful hallways – that feels like my most iconic look today. I felt like I've never felt before."

    Mayra Carreño

    What is your ideal getting-ready set up?

    "My ideal getting-ready set up would be having everything organised, efficient and pretty minimal, as I don't love spending the most amount of time getting ready – apart from when I get really glammed up. I enjoy that process because I don't do it so often."

    When do you feel most beautiful?

    "I feel most beautiful when I've had a good sleep. And honestly, for me, skincare is a huge part of it. When I have clear skin, I don't feel the need to wear any make-up and being in the sun really helps. I love having clear skin so I can go out with no real preparation."

    Mayra Carreño

    Fashion has a reputation for being frivolous, but why does what we wear matter?

    "I really believe that what we wear matters as it's an expression of how we're feeling or the type of personality that we are. It's a really creative way to express yourself, so I enjoy playing with different styles depending on my mood, the city I'm in or the weather – it can be a really fun thing to experiment with."

    The Mike Ward Interview with Talksport's Paul Hawksbee and Andy Jacobs

    Here I am, chatting away with talk Sport legends Paul Hawksbee and Andy Jacobs about the new football season, about this summers sportinghighlights, about their soon-to-be 20 years on air when Andy suddenly heaves out a bin bag.

    21 Feb 2024, 00:00

    Here I am, chatting away with talk Sport legends Paul Hawksbee and Andy Jacobs – about the new football season, about this summer’s sporting highlights, about their soon-to-be 20 years on air – when Andy suddenly heaves out a bin bag.

    I’m thinking: blimey, what the hell’s in there? His household refuse? A service wash?

    Nope, turns out it’s some of his artwork.

    “I thought you might like to see this,” he goes.

    And do you know what? He’s right. It’s mad but I love it.

    The piece he fishes out for me is his own quirky take on Panini football stickers, aping their style but with his own surreal twist: legends such as Terry Butcher and Diego Maradona depicted as never before.

    On his phone he shows me other stuff he’s done, mimicking great artists: Dali, Hockney, even Rembrandt. I particularly like his Beatles doodle. And Jimmy Hill in the style of Picasso.

    Creating this art, says Andy, is “my new passion. I’ve become quite obsessed.

    “And what’s surprising is I never knew I had it in me.”

    Apparently, it was only about a year ago that Andy, at the age of 65, became aware of this latent gift of his. While on air he’d started randomly doodling – but with way more distinctive results than he’d ever known he was capable of.

    “It was a really weird sensation, I can’t explain it. Even Paul was impressed. And he’s not the easiest person to please.”

    Having honed his style further (“I started taking them home to colour in…”) he’s now selling these pictures as limited-edition signed prints, in aid of Willow, the charity for seriously ill young adults.

    But it sounds as if he took some persuasion. “I’m never going to go, ‘Oh, I’m fantastic,’” he says, “but if others like them, that’s great.”

    Andy and Paul are different personalities in many ways, but this unassuming attitude is one trait they share, as listeners to their weekday afternoon show will testify. “Some people like to shout the odds,” says Andy, “but one thing we have in common is we’re quite self-deprecating.”

    Paul takes it as a compliment when their show is likened to two blokes just chatting down the pub. “We do sometimes go down these pub-style conversational cul-de-sacs,” he chuckles. “Then during the ad break I’ll turn to Andy and go, ‘Remind me, what was yours, a half of Stella..?’”

    But he knows that’s half their appeal. “Listeners tell us, ‘I got in my car and I don’t remember much of the journey,’” he says. “And that’s great. We like to help people travel from A to B as painlessly as possible.”

    Of course, they’ve had plenty of talking points this summer. The FIFA Women’s World Cup, for example. A game-changer?

    “I’m not sure,” says Andy. “But I’ll tell you one thing it did throw up: I suddenly realised I couldn’t be as funny/rude about the women as I am about the men.

    “To do a Hawksbee and Jacobs-style show about women’s football, you’d have to be a woman. Otherwise it would sound sexist and horrible."

    Paul agrees, but does think some irreverence would be welcome: “There are people doing good stuff about women’s football but obviously they’re banging the drum for the sport. So someone looking at it with a slightly jaundiced eye wouldn’t be a bad thing.”

    VAR, too, is obviously a biggy now. Having initially been “quite pro”, both now have reservations. “I do think it’ll settle down and have a lot of positives,” says Paul, “but as an invention it’s a joysucker.”

    Elsewhere, of course, England’s one-day cricketers took the world title, in that last-gasp triumph over the Kiwis at Lord’s. Cricket-mad Andy must have been thrilled.

    “It was incredible,” he says. “I mean, obviously it did beg some questions about the rules. There were things England really shouldn’t have been able to profit from.

    “But on the other hand we won, so who cares?!”

    An attitude summing up Hawksbee and Jacobs to a T.

    So as their 20th anniversary looms, are there any big plans?

    “Not really,” shrugs Paul. “Maybe just keeping us on air…”

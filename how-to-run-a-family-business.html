    How to run a family business

    “We were sitting down at Olympia’s flat and we thought: why don’t we just do this together?” Carolina Irving and her daughters, Olympia and Ariadne, are recalling the moment in 2018 that they decided to found their business together as a family. “It wasn’t something that was premeditated at all,” says Carolina. Two years later, the lifestyle-focused Carolina Irving & Daughters was born: a hub for art-inspired homeware, with a particular focus on tabletop pieces, it’s now a go-to for the style-set.

    A textile designer by trade, Carolina has years of experience in craftsmanship, and has also worked for the likes of designers such as Oscar de la Renta and Penny Morrison. “My time at Oscar de la Renta taught me a lot,” she says, “because I was there when the brand developed homeware, which was a very good exercise”. At the time the trio decided to found their business, Ariadne was at Cabana magazine and Olympia was working for Robin Birley at 5 Hertford Street. “We were all working across design and entertaining, which encompasses what our brand is at its heart,” says Ariadne.

    Play to your strengths

    Now, the brand stocks everything from hand-painted plates designed by the founders and made in Portugal, to shell-shaped candlesticks, hand-blown Egyptian glassware and printed linens from India. “When the girls were growing up – their father is an art dealer – they were always surrounded by beautiful things,” explains Carolina. “And the table was always exquisitely laid. My mother, too, had that obsession with the table: it’s an environment I grew up in, and they grew up in, too.”

    Running a family business has, for the Irvings, been a fruitful experience. “For me, as a mother, it’s a dream come true,” laughs Carolina, and her children agree – “there’s no one else I trust more than my mum and sister, and that’s the most important thing,” says Ariadne. “When you’re working for other people, at some point you have to be polite, whereas we only create the things we know we want.”

    From left to right: Caroline, Ariadne and Olympia Irving, who founded their eponymous business in 2018

    Know your place within the business

    They have a system, however, to ensure that the company runs smoothly; an idea they’d recommend to anyone who’s thinking of starting a business with their own family. “We all know our place,” says Olympia. “I know that I’m better at logistics and the things that are more on the business side, whereas my mum loves researching the tiny design details. Figuring out your places in the business is crucial, otherwise, it’s a case of too many cooks in the kitchen.”

    Listen to everyone's perspective

    Working inter-generationally also brings a valuable, dual perspective to the company, which caters for both design veterans in Carolina’s mould, and her younger daughters’ cohort. “We bring the young side – social media, the website, videos and so on – whereas mum has this vast knowledge of the decorative arts that puts us a step ahead of all the other companies doing the same thing,” explains Olympia. “It’s easy to get stuck designing the same things over and over again, but every so often, the girls will come to me and say: ‘Listen, why don’t we do something a bit fresher?’” adds Carolina. “It makes me look at things in a completely different way.”

    Sanda Vuckovic

    Olympia perfecting a table in the trio’s signature style

    Have respect for one another

    Of course, running a company together means that the professional and the personal often bleed into one another – especially as what the family designs and sells is so close to their hearts. “Our jobs are our lives!” laughs Olympia. “But we’re also lucky, because the business we’ve started is so ingrained in our lives anyway. Our WhatsApp group is terrifying, though – we must have sent about 40 thousand images in the past few years!”

    Their ultimate rule, however, for running a family company successfully? “There has to be a lot of respect,” says Ariadne. “You love each other, but you have to like each other too. When you’re working with family, you want the best for them – no matter what.”

    The Coca-Cola company’s ingenious marketing strategy has seen it grow to become one of the world’s biggest brands. The genesis and evolution of the firm are fascinating. It is all attributable to its exciting collection of variable beverage flavours, customised to suit the tastes and preferences of different market niches globally. No other product in the company’s line has seen as much development and variation as the Fanta brand. With so many Fanta flavors, you cannot help but wonder - how many more are out there?

    One thing about the Coca-Cola beverage company is that it is hard to beat them in marketing. They are so aggressive that their products are famous worldwide and are now a profound brand. Fanta is one of the firm’s best beverages and its most successful in history.

    Thanks to their skillful advertising efforts, Fanta has had numerous changes, with the company developing different flavours. So, how many Fanta flavors are there??

    List of all Fanta flavors sold globally

    The Coca-Cola Company is always out to discover the next big thing with its always-on-point beverage flavours.

    The firm has played around with Fanta’s flavours, giving consumers a different taste and something they have always yearned for. So, what Fanta flavours are there?

    1. Fanta Orange

    Fanta’s origin story might surprise you. Did you know the flavourful soft drink gained popularity in Germany when the world was about to enter a global war? Then, it was a wartime drink made from leftovers, far from what it is today.

    In the post-war years of the 1950s, Fanta became the fruity, delicious beverage people love today. The Fanta Orange flavour from the Coca-Cola company is rich in history as it is one of the oldest flavours of the internationally-renowned brand.

    Fanta was born in Naples, Italy, in 1955, utilising local citrus to create the now famous orange flavour. Today, Fanta Orange, the original Fanta flavour, is one of the most popular and sold in virtually every part of the world.

    2. Fanta Grape

    Fanta Grape is one of the most popular Fanta flavors in the USA. It was first released in the United States of America in 1960 and has been sold ever since. The flavour gained immense popularity during its first years of production and has remained one of the firm’s most robust and reliable products.

    3. Fanta Strawberry

    Who invented Fanta Strawberry? Like other flavours in Coca-Cola’s exciting line-up of beverages, Fanta Strawberry has an interesting story. The flavour was released in the United States of America in 1962, two years after Fanta Grape and other Fanta originals were released.

    Consumers fell in love with the sweet flavour, and its popularity grew immediately. In 2002, the flavours got intensely promoted by spokesladies named The Fantanas. They promoted the firm’s four core Fanta flavours, which include Fanta Orange, Fanta Grape, Fanta Strawberry, and Fanta Pineapple.

    4. Fanta Pineapple

    Fanta Pineapple is one of the most popular Fanta flavors around the world, on top of other world-renowned flavours like orange, grape, and strawberry. The flavour was first released in 1987 in Japan and gained positive feedback from consumers during its first few years.

    The massive acceptance from consumers in Japan made the Coca-Cola company interested in introducing it to other markets. And in 1989, Fanta Pineapple made its way to the United States of America and has been one of the most recognised and reliable flavours for over 30 years.

    5. Fanta Berry

    The Coca-Cola Company is not proud of Fanta Berry’s history, considering its release and subsequent discontinuation two years later. The flavour was released in August 2002 as a permanent flavour in the United States of America. Unfortunately, sales were consistently low, and it was discontinued in 2004, around the same time Pepsi Blue got axed.

    The Coca-Cola Company revived the flavour and released it once again in 2015 to grow its Fanta line-up in the United States of America. Fanta Berry has strawberries and blueberries, but the flavour needs to be distinguishable.

    6. Fanta Wild Cherry

    When was Fanta Wild Cherry released? The famous brand was released as a freeze/soda fountain exclusive flavour. The flavour combined the refreshing fruit notes with a sharp edge, giving consumers something extraordinary.

    In 2019, Fanta Wild Cherry got released as an exclusive flavour in cans and bottles. These days, people might misinterpret the flavour as Fanta Cherry.

    7. Fanta Fruit Punch

    Coca-Cola released Fanta Fruit Punch as a Freestyle exclusive flavour. The firm released 12-pack cans in 2009 and later released bottles in 2016. The Fanta website describes the flavours as “an upbeat flavour party moment to remember kick if with friends punch up the fruits and stay true to yourself.”

    8. Fanta Melon Soda

    Fanta Melon Soda is one of Fanta Japan’s best flavours. After a brief absence, the flavour was revived in PET bottle form, and the aluminium bottle seems to have disappeared.

    As the name suggests, the flavour has a profound melon flavour like no other, and it is perfectly balanced with the right amount of carbonation. However, the bottle’s graphic occasionally changes.

    9. Fanta White Peach

    Fanta White Peach is a well-known Japanese flavour now available in cans. However, it is a limited edition that is hard to get. The flavour’s distinct taste makes it even more appealing to consumers.

    10. Fanta Golden Apple Power

    Have you ever tasted the Fanta Golden Apple and Power? You should. It is an exciting flavour that gives you the sweet taste you are looking for in a beverage and has vitamin ingredients that are good for your health.

    11. Fanta Furu Furu Shaker

    Fanta Furu Furu shaker first went on sale in 2008. It has an interesting concept as you can only enjoy the carbonated drink if you shake it. The shaking ideology made the drink an instant success upon its release as it gave consumers something exciting and fun aside from conventional drinking with a straw.

    12. Fanta Japanese Citrus

    Fanta Japanese Citrus is made from the sweet-tasting iyokan citrus, indigenous to Japan. Once you open this incredible flavour, you enjoy a zesty citrus aroma like no other.

    13. Fanta Shine Muscat

    The packaging of the Fanta Shine Muscat flavour is out of this world. At first glance, you will fall in love with the bottle, even before opening it to enjoy the sweet aroma of vibrant flavour. Shine muscat is a popular fruit in Japan.

    14. Fanta Peach

    Fanta Peach is another popular Fanta flavour in Japan. Without a doubt, white peaches are sweet, and now, imagine it in your favourite beverage. Wouldn't you have it every day? The flavour smells like candied peaches and has a fantastic bubble gum pink pattern.

    15. Fanta Salty Lychee

    The Fanta Salty Lychee was introduced in 2019. It is a limited edition made from the sweet tropical fruit lychee that creates the perfect flavour combination. Try this during summer; you will love how it counteracts the summer heat like it was meant to be.

    16. Fanta Okinawa Shikuwasa Fruit

    The Fanta Okinawa Sikuwas Fruit flavour is only available in Okinawa, Japan. Its limited availability has made numerous people interested in its unique flavour. The beverage has a distinct energising citrus flavour from the Shikuwasa citrus fruit.

    17. Fanta Instamix

    The Fanta Instamix is Japan’s attempt at a flavoured syrup inspired by European cuisine. One thing to expect once you sip this favourite flavour is that you will not taste anything like it. And the most exciting thing is that you can choose from four tastes by purchasing two 500ml bottles.

    What flavors of Fanta are in Kenya?

    Fanta Orange, Fanta black currant, and Fanta Pineapple are popular brands in Kenya. The Coca-Cola company recently launched a new flavour, encouraging consumers to taste and guess.     What is the rarest Fanta flavor?

    The rarest Fanta flavour is the Fanta Okinawa Shikuwasa Fruit flavour. It is only available in Okinawa, Japan, which means most people need to learn how it tastes.

    Mario Batali Apologizes to Friends and Family After Admitting to Sexual Misconduct

    27 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Celebrity chef Mario Batali has publicly apologized to his “friends and family” after admitting to sexual misconduct allegations made by multiple women who previously worked with The Chew star. “I apologize to the people I have mistreated and hurt. Although the identities of most of the individuals mentioned in these stories have not been revealed to me, much of the behavior described does, in fact, match up with ways I have acted,” he said in a statement on Monday, Dec. 11. “That behavior was wrong and there are no excuses. I take full responsibility and am deeply sorry for any pain, humiliation or discomfort I have caused to my peers, employees, customers, friends, and family.”

    He continued, “I know my actions have disappointed many people. The successes I have enjoyed are owned by everyone on my team. The failures are mine alone. To the people who have been at my side during this time — my family, my partners, my employees, my friends, my fans — I am grateful for your support and hopeful that I can regain your respect and trust. I will spend the next period of time trying to do that.” Following Mario’s The Chew firing and scandal, many fans have wondered about his life off-screen — scroll down to learn more about Mario.

    Is Mario Batali married?

    Yes, Mario has been married to Susi Cahn for 23 years. They previously tied the knot in 1994. Susi works for Coach Dairy Goat Farm in Pine Plains, NY. Mario’s in-laws are Miles and Lillian Cahn, who are the co-founders of Coach, Inc. Mario and Susi now live in Greenwich Village, NYC and they have a summer home on Grand Traverse Bay in Michigan.

    Does Mario Batali have kids?

    Yes, Mario and his wife Susi are parents to two kids, sons Leo and Benno Batali.

    What is Mario Batali’s net worth?

    In 2015, the Daily Mail estimated Mario’s net worth to be around $30 million. In addition to being a celebrity chef, Mario is a writer, restaurateur, and co-host of The Chew on ABC. He currently co-owns Italian restaurants in NYC, Las Vegas, LA, Boston, Singapore, Westport, CT, and New Haven, CT. As of 2017, Mario has authored 13 cookbooks including The Babbo Cookbook (2002), Molto Italiano (2005), Molto Gusto: Easy Italian Cooking (2010), and America Farm to Table: Simple, Delicious Recipes Celebrating Local Farmers (2014), just to name a few!

    Mario with his sons and wife in 2013.

    Did Mario Batali leave The Chew?

    Yes, Mario was fired from The Chew in December 2017 following allegations of sexual misconduct. The chef later confessed to a pattern of inappropriate behavior after four women accused him of sexual misconduct in an article for Eater published on Monday, Dec. 11. The allegations spanned at least two decades, and three of the accusers have worked for The Chew co-host in the past.

    In a statement to People magazine, ABC revealed, “We have asked Mario Batali to step away from The Chew while we review the allegations that have just recently come to our attention. ABC takes matters like this very seriously as we are committed to a safe work environment. While we are unaware of any type of inappropriate behavior involving him and anyone affiliated with the show, we will swiftly address any alleged violations of our standards of conduct.”

    Meanwhile, the Screen Actors Guild Award winner recently suffered a blow in court by LA Superior Court Judge Holly Kendig. 

    Sheridan claimed in a previous 2012 trial that Desperate Housewives creator and Executive Producer Marc Cherry had deliberately hit her in 2008 while on set as the pair feuded over a scene. 

    She reported the incident to network heads and maintained that it resulted in her being 'killed' off from the hit ABC series.

    Tied up: Nicollette swept her signature silky blonde tresses down and to one side which she knotted in a neat twisted bun

    Cherry testified that it was decided to kill off her character, Eddie Britt, before the occurrence and said the termination has nothing to do with the incident.

    'The Court finds that the Defendant has presented substantial undisputed evidence of the creative decision to kill off Sheridan’s character, developed over several seasons of the show,' Kendig wrote last month in a ruling which granted a motion for summary judgment by Touchstone TV in Nicollette's claim of retaliation against the ABC division.

    She added: 'Thus… Touchstone’s legitimate reasons for killing off Sheridan’s character would have resulted in the same decision. Sheridan has not presented a triable issue of fact showing that retaliatory animus was a "substantial motivating factor" of the employment decision.'

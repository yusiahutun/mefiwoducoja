    The Crosby family played a decisive role in shaping his life and career. His family has been the love, support, and guidance on every step of his journey, starting when he began to play sports at a very young age before achieving success in college and the National Football League.

    High school & College career

    When growing up, Crosby was always interested in sports, especially football. Before transferring to Mackinaw City High School in Michigan, he played for his high school team at Colleyville Heritage High School in Texas.

    He excelled as a football and basketball player at Mackinaw City, where he was named an All-Conference Player in both sports.

    In his college years, Crosby’s love for football continued, and he played at Eastern Michigan University. At the college, he quickly established himself as one of the Eagles’ most important players and earned All-MAC honors twice.

    NFL Career

    Maxx Crosby’s NFL career began when the Las Vegas Raiders selected him in the fourth round of the 2019 NFL Draft.

    He quickly became a key player on the Raiders’ defense, playing in all 16 games in his rookie season and starting in 10.

    Crosby’s performance in his rookie year was impressive, as he recorded ten sacks, 16 tackles for loss, and four forced fumbles.

    His ten bags were the most by a Raiders rookie since 2011, earning him a spot on the NFL All-Rookie Team.

    Crosby’s success continued during the second year when he recorded seven sacks, 14 tackles for loss, and three forced fumbles. 

    After recording five tackles, two sacks, and three for loss against the Kansas City Chiefs in Week 11 of the 2020 season, he was named the AFC Defensive Player of the Week.

    On March 11, 2022, Crosby extended his four-year contract with the Raiders.

    Who Is The Wife Of Maxx Crosby?

    Maxx Crosby’s Addiction to Drugs & Alcohol

    Crosby has been open about his struggles with addiction in the past. In college, he was suspended for violating the school’s substance abuse policy and sought treatment for his addiction.

    Since then, he has been very vocal about his journey to sobriety and has become an advocate for others struggling with addiction.

    In an interview with ESPN in 2020, Crosby discussed his past struggles with addiction, saying, “I’m not ashamed of it. It’s part of my story. I’m open, honest, and proud of how far I’ve come.”
